\section{Gemini Mountains}

\emph{Present Day, 1991, the small town of Gemini Mountains.  Can a struggling company of paranormal investigators locate a lost lover while evading corporately-cursed clothing?  Tune in to find out!  Thursdays 8PM Eastern/7PM Central on ABC.  PG-13.}\\

    The Curse of \ai is a Genesys adventure for 3-5 Player Characters (PCs) with a typical playtime of 3 hours.  Inspired by 1990s supernatural television shows like \emph{Twin Peaks}, \emph{Buffy The Vampire Slayer}, \emph{The X-Files}, and others, the setting is mundane except the PCs and select adversaries are familiar with supernatural forces that lurk beneath public awareness.

\section{Adventure Overview}

    The PCs are a band of paranomal investigators/exterminators hired by Andre to search for his missing boyfriend, Breaux.  In doing so, they uncover Breaux's connection to Advanced Biotech Corporation (ABC) and the heavily marketed \ai clothing product line.  The PCs will find that deploying \ai has some serious consequences for everyone involved.\\
\\
\emph{Goal}: Discover what happened to Breaux and uncover the Curse of \ai.\\
\\
\emph{Challenge}: (spoiler) \ai is a supernatural clothing product that requires skill ``donations'' to grant those skills to the wearer.  Breaux fell into debt using their products unsuccessfully, so ABC has ``repossessed'' him to work that off.  \ai clothes also tend to go haywire and operate without a wearer.\\
\\
\emph{Narrative Reminder}: (spoiler) \ai adversaries show a degree of autonomy and malice, but not \emph{sentience}.  They display no pain when damaged nor joy when successful.  Play them similarly to modern artificial intelligence models, which can act chaotically and without regard for logic.\\
\emph{Author's Note}: While \ai is directly inspired by modern artificial intellgence models, this adventure is entirely human written and edited.

\section{Title Sequence}

To help players visualize the setting and get into character, start with an almost literal title sequence of a news piece about/ABC ad for \ai.  Read the italicized text:

\emph{The camera zooms in on a reporter interviewing the winner of the local Gemini Mountains marathon, an unfamiliar woman wearing bright blue leggings.  She is frenetic, unable to stand still as she explains, ``A week ago I could barely run a 5k, but now I'm a champion runner.  Thanks \ai!''  An awkward smile crosses her face as she stutters a goodbye and starts a victory lap, seemingly unable to stop her legs from running.}

\emph{The camera briefly pans over to another marathoner, exhausted and drained, leaning against a wall.  You recognize her as the previous three-time marathon winner in town.  Smash cut to the character intro sequences.}

Ask one of the PCs to briefly describe their introductory sequence as though it were television show.  This should be a brief shot of the character doing something key to their concept, without dialogue, and unrelated to any specific adventure.  After they describe their character, ask the next player and so on.

\emph{Example Introduction 1}: Candra the witch is sitting cross-legged in a pentagram.  Five candles sit unlit at each point of the star.  Reflected in Candra's eyes, we see a candle ignite.

\emph{Example Introduction 2}: Sebastian the engineer is typing furiously at a bulky 1990s laptop computer.  The camera rotates around the scene to show ``Ghost Program Activated'' on the screen as Sebastian grins.

After all characters have introduced themselves, the show title appears, displaying,``The Curse of \ai''.  You may optionally ``cut to a commercial break'' here to further immerse players in the television aesthetic.

\section{Investigators For Hire}

Andre, a well-dressed lawyer, enters the PCs' paranormal extermination office during his lunch break.  If the PCs have not yet named their business, ask them for a name now.  He is clearly distressed or in a hurry as he steps inside.

    Andre wants to hire the PCs to find and bring back his missing ``roommate'' and ``friend'', Breaux (pronounced "bro").  He can pay a significant amount up front (the PCs desperately need the income, or if part of a long-running campaign, some other information/object/favor from Andre) and is looking to close a deal quickly.  Andre explains that he needs paranormal investigators because Breaux was last seen heading to his haunted workshop several days ago.  Andre is terrified of ghosts and refuses to go there.

    PCs should first attempt to elicit more details from Andre before making a \textbf{Negotiation} check to close the deal.  Many different approaches are reasonable and offer different benefits (narrative or mechanical), so here are few examples to explain Andre's situation.

    \df\df \textbf{Leadership} to ask for more detail & Breaux has been working on an art project at the workshop and making many purchases at a local hardware store.\\
    \df\df \textbf{Perception} to observe Andre & He keeps glancing at his watch and avoids looking at paranormal objects in the shop.  +\bbb to \textbf{Negotiate}\\

Let each PC make one such informational roll (if they want).  Then the group must make a single \ch\df\df\textbf{Negotiation} check to sign the investigation contract with Andre.  They're going to get the job either way, but the results can influence immediate (and if appropriate, campaign level) benefits/hindrances:

\begin{itemize}
    \setlength\itemsep{-.5em}

        \item \Failure: Andre signs the contract, but it's not enough to keep the business afloat long.  +\stb to all social interactions with townspeople for various debts stil owed.
        \item \Advantage\Advantage: Every two advantages constitute profit they can use to use for +\bbb on future social checks or longer term needs.
        \item \Threat\Threat: Andre leaves in a hurry and forgets to give the PCs a picture of Breaux to assist the investigation.  They'll have to make do with a generic description of ``tall, short black hair, very good-looking.  I mean, umm, conventionally attractive.''
        \item \Threat\Threat\Threat: Andre is concerned about secrecy and insists that the PCs avoid disclosing that they are searching for Breaux.
        \item \Triumph: Andre gives the PCs one of Breaux work gloves. It is bright blue and shows the logo of ABC's \ai.
        \item \Despair: Andre also hires a more traditional private investigator that will compete and interfere with the PCs' efforts.  If necessary, use stats similar to the PCs, except substitute more mundane skills for anything supernatural.
    \end{itemize}

After signing the contract, Andre leaves quickly to get back to work.  The PCs have picture of Breaux and Andre together from a mall photobooth kiosk as well as the address for Breaux's haunted workshop.  While Andre cannot immediately go with the PCs, if they find strong evidence that Breaux is at the ABC facility, he may accompany them there if pressed with a social check.  He does not have his own stat block but rather can upgrade PCs social and \textbf{Mundane} checks with his legal knowledge.

\section{Haunted Workshop}

Up a windy mountain road, the PCs can find Breaux's workshop.  The lights are out, the doors are locked, and a car is haphazardly parked in the dirt in front of the garage.  They might want to start investigating the outside for any evidence.  Many different skills are possible, but a \df\df \textbf{Perception} check is a good start.  Add +\stb if it's already dark.  Tailor the results to the specifics of what the PCs are interested in:

\vspace{-1em}
\begin{itemize}
    \setlength\itemsep{-.5em}
    \item \Success: The car was parked here within the last 24 hours, more recently than the last Breaux sighting.
    \item \Advantage\Advantage: There are no sign of human footprints, but there are scurrying marks from an unidentifiable creature leading to the window.
    \item \Threat\Threat: It is so cold that locks and doors are frozen.  Add \stb to checks to break into the workshop.
    \item \Triumph: The PCs find a torn piece of an \ai Trenchcoat.  Roll a Critical Injury that the Trenchcoat starts combat with.
\end{itemize}
\vspace{-1em}

As the workshop is locked, the PCs will have to find some way inside.  They can try picking a lock, climbing through a tight window, forcing a door, or something more creative.  Once one PC succeeds, they can let the others in without issue.  You can spend \Advantage and \Triumph similar to in the earlier \textbf{Perception} check if they haven't already learned that information.  They could also turn on the lights or other workshop tools.  \Threat\Threat could impede searching inside the workshop or the initiative check for when the \ai adversaries attack.  Depending on the approach taken, offer an extra bonus as shown below.

\begin{GenesysTable}{p{1.5in}p{2in}}
    Approach & \\
    \df\df\df \textbf{Athletics} to force the front door & Gain entry and a wooden stake (+1 dmg, Melee, Engaged, Crit 3, Vicious 1).\\
    \df\df\df \textbf{Coordination} to squeeze through a small window & Gain entry and easy access to power tools.\\
    \df\df\stb \textbf{Skulduggery} to pick a door lock & Gain entry and a set of keys to Breaux's car. \\
\end{GenesysTable}\\

If the PCs fail all these checks, still let them in, but with no bonus.  Also add +\stb to all subsequent checks as it darkens further.  The town clocktower strikes an hour later.

Once inside, the PCs can look for clues to Breaux's whereabouts or the supposed hauntings.  Even without looking, they can see things typical to a workshop or art studio.  A beautiful landscape painting is nearly complete in one corner.  Scrap lumber is in a pile in another.  Various power tools like a table saw and drill press sit idle.  Cabinets and closets sit half-closed along the wall.  The area is well-used, but not in disarray.  The whole space is the size of a large apartment; it is \textbf{Medium} range from one end to the other.  Some things they might find:

\begin{GenesysTable}{p{1.5in}p{2in}}

    \df\df \textbf{Perception} to see what does not belong. & Signs of struggle near the far closet.  No blood, but discarded tools and an ABC pamphlet in an otherwise orderly workshop.\\

\section{Entering ABC Facility}

The letter was postmarked only yesterday, so the ABC facility is the latest lead on where Breaux is.  It's back in town in a plain industrial park, but fenced off.  They first need to gain access to the grounds; the sleepy security guard staffing the gate is one option, but be open to PC creativity (e.g. posing as legitimate employees or complaining customers).

\begin{GenesysTable}{p{1.5in}p{2in}}

    \df\df\stb \textbf{Charm/Negotiate} to ask the guard to let them in & Gain legitimate entry, but interior guards aware of PCs presence.\\
    \ch\df\bbb \textbf{Stealth} to sneak by the guard & Gain entry without alerting ABC.\\
    \df\df\stb \textbf{Coordination} to climb fence & Gain entry but leave obvious mud tracks. \\

The ABC \ai Skill Donation Facility is a three story office and lab building with a front space devoted to retail.  A few lights are on, and the front door is actually unlocked (\ai Trenchcoats need to be able to drag uncooperative volunteers through easily).  Other doors require \df\df\stb\stb \textbf{Skulduggery} check to open and will enter directly to the hallway where Breaux is.

The front retail area where PCs are likely to enter is currently unstaffed.  Dim lights illuminate a variety of \ai products like gloves, leggings, and sunglasses, but no trenchcoats.  There are also some informational brochures and building layout information.  This is a good chance for PCs to learn more about the technology behind \ai as well as at least the ``skill donation'' hallway Breaux is likely to be in.  Natural choices for learning this info is a \df\df \textbf{Science} or \df\df \textbf{Mundane} check.  Other PCs might want to assist by using \df\df \textbf{Perception} to look for relevant materials.  Things they can learn are:

\begin{itemize}
    \setlength\itemsep{-.5em}

    \item \Success: \ai requires humans to ``donate'' skills in a supernatural ritual, leaving them drained of those skills for some time.
    \item \Advantage\Advantage: Two \ai Trenchcoats and a human security guard are listed as on-duty for this building.
    \item \Threat\Threat: PCs catch a momentary glimpse of an \ai Glove skittering under the door to the stairs they need to take.  They each suffer 1 Strain.
    \item \Triumph: \ai products wear out quickly by design to encourage subscription.  PCs learn their weaknesses and deal +1 damage to all \ai adversaries.
\end{itemize}

If they intervene, however, they will have a social or physical encounter with the guard. \section{Breaux's Sacrifice}

A system of adjustable flags above doors indicates which rooms are currently in use for skill draining.  Besides the marathoner's, only one other is active.  That is Breaux's room.

Listening at the door reveals a man's sobbing voice repeating, ``I'm so sorry, I'm so sorry!'' with no check required.  When they open the door (or make a sufficient ruckus just outside it), however, they see a man dressed in multiple pieces of \ai holding a wicked-looked knife over a person strapped to the examination table.  He is about to strike them.

The PCs have but a moment to act, but when the man turns, he is recognizable as Breaux, despite wearing \ai gloves, some kind of \ai robe, and even a long-haired \ai wig.  His face is red from sobbing.

The PCs may not realize, but Breaux is the ``cult maniac'', though against his will as well.  The \ai wig is controlling his physical actions.  The marathoner, if present, will try to attack Breaux, not realizing or caring about the distinction.  Otherwise, she can add +\bbb to one PC's roll each round of combat.

In addition to Breaux himself, hiding in a closet in the room is another \ai Trenchcoat.  It attempts to attack a dangerous PC and enwrap them.  Its eventual goal is to drag one of them to an empty examination room across the hall.  If there are 4 or more PCs, add a second \ai Trenchcoat as well.  Along with Breaux's extra turn, this makes for 3 or 4 adversary turns in a round, which should be a challenging combat encounter.

Each of Breaux's pieces of \ai gear grants him specific skills or abilities.  See his character block for details.  PCs can attempt to target specific pieces for removal (gloves or wig) or destruction (robes or wig).  Follow the standard rules for targeted attacks by required an Aim maneuver and add +\stb\stb to the check.  Additionally, removing the \ai Wig first requires an opposed \textbf{Coordination} check followed by an opposed \textbf{Athletics} check on a later turn.  Attack rolls to damage the \ai Wig also upgrade the difficulty an extra time to reflect the danger of hitting Breaux himself on the head.

If the PCs roll \Despair or \Threat\Threat\Threat on these (or they incapacitate him), then Breaux is mortally wounded.  A PC must attempt to stabilize him with a \df\df\df \textbf{Medicine} check.  Other PCs can assist by scrounging for supplies in the examination room (\df\df \textbf{Perception}) or applying some relevant arcane knowledge with \df\df \textbf{Supernatural}.  Each such successful check can upgrade the ability of medical stabilization (being potentially more effective then a generic Aid Another maneuver).

If the PCs either remove the \ai Wig or incapacitate Breaux, the fight is effectively over.  The \ai Trenchcoat and any remaining adversaries will flee.  Nothing directly hinders PCs escape, but if they start to linger, trigger an alarm to encourage them to seek safety.

Andre is relieved to see Breaux again as they both confess their love for each other.  If PCs have some specific evidence about ABC's wrongdoings, he will offer to start legal proceedings against them, although this will take some time.  Similarly, the marathoner or victim on Breaux's table can assist the PCs in future adventures (or become a new PC if someone joins later) acting against ABC.  Most mundane people want nothing to do with the supernatural, so every ally they can gather is valuable.

\section{Aftermath}

    This is a narrative epilogue for the ``viewers'' of the television program, with the PCs remaining unaware of these details (until a possible future adventure).  If an \ai Trenchcoat escaped from the final combat, the camera follows it fleeing to an administrative ABC building.  Otherwise, a fresh one from another examination room fills a similar role.  In a dimly-lit executive office, the coat pantomimes the PCs' rescue of Breaux to the back of an expensive high-backed office chair.

    The chair slowly rotates, revealing a stern woman in business-casual attire (notably, \emph{not} bright blue like \ai products).  Showing little emotion, she calmly snaps her fingers.  The trenchcoat bursts into flame, continuing to flail even as it turns to ash.  The camera zooms out and the viewers see a wall of supernatural artifacts.  As it pulls beyond the office door, the executive's name placard becomes visible, identifying her as, ``Cillarma Tepes, Chief Arcane Officer.''

If you continue in this campaign setting, Cillarma can be a long-term adversary, wielding both mundane corporate resources and supernatural abilities against the PCs and their allies.  ``Cillarma'' anagrams to ``Carmilla'', one of the earliest vampires popularized in English fiction.  Adapt her background (and name) as needed to your campaign.
